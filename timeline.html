Significant Events

Pact of Biak-na-Bato (December 14, 1897)

The Pact of Biak-na-Bato (December 14, 1897) was an agreement between the Spanish colonial government and Filipino revolutionaries led by Emilio Aguinaldo. Under this pact, Aguinaldo and other revolutionary leaders agreed to go into exile in Hong Kong in exchange for a promised payment of ₱800,000 and reforms from Spain. The agreement temporarily stopped the Philippine Revolution, but Spain failed to fully implement the promised reforms, causing tensions to continue.

Battle of Manila Bay (May 1, 1898)

The Battle of Manila Bay (May 1, 1898) was a major naval battle during the Spanish-American War. The American fleet, commanded by George Dewey, decisively defeated the Spanish navy in Manila Bay. This victory destroyed Spain’s naval power in the Philippines and marked the beginning of the end of Spanish colonial rule in the country.

Aguinaldo Returned to the Philippines (May 19, 1898)

On May 19, 1898, Emilio Aguinaldo returned to the Philippines from exile in Hong Kong with the assistance of the United States. Upon his return, he reorganized the Filipino revolutionary forces and resumed the fight against Spain. His leadership strengthened the revolution and led to significant victories in different provinces.

Philippine Independence (June 12, 1898)

Philippine Independence was proclaimed on June 12, 1898, in Kawit, Cavite. The declaration was prepared by Ambrosio Rianzares Bautista and publicly announced by Aguinaldo. During the ceremony, the Philippine flag was officially unfurled for the first time, and the national anthem was played. Aguinaldo became the first President of the Philippines, symbolizing the country’s freedom from Spanish rule.

Mock Battle of Manila (August 13, 1898)

The Mock Battle of Manila (August 13, 1898) was a staged battle secretly arranged between Spain and the United States. Although it appeared to be a real fight, it was planned so that Spain could surrender Manila to American forces without allowing Filipino revolutionaries to take control of the city. This event marked the official end of Spanish rule in Manila but also excluded Filipino forces from victory.

Treaty of Paris (December 10, 1898)

The Treaty of Paris (December 10, 1898) was signed between Spain and the United States, officially ending the Spanish-American War. Under the treaty, Spain ceded the Philippines to the United States for $20 million. This agreement transferred control of the Philippines from Spain to the U.S., leading to a new period of American colonization.

Philippine-American War (1899–1902)

The Philippine-American War (1899–1902) began on February 4, 1899, after tensions rose between Filipino forces and American troops. Filipinos fought to defend their independence, but the war resulted in heavy casualties and widespread destruction. In the end, the United States emerged victorious and established control over the Philippines.

Capture of Aguinaldo in Palanan, Isabela (March 23, 1901)

On March 23, 1901, Aguinaldo was captured in Palanan, Isabela by American forces led by Frederick Funston. His capture significantly weakened the Filipino resistance. Afterward, Aguinaldo took an oath of allegiance to the United States, which marked a turning point in the struggle against American rule.
